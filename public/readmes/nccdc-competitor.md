## National CCDC Competitor
**Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition**
*2023 – Present*

I compete at the national level in the Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition, defending enterprise infrastructure against professional red team operators composed of industry security experts.

This experience has been the single most impactful part of my cybersecurity education. No classroom exercise, certification, or lab environment replicates the intensity of defending a live network against skilled attackers who are actively trying to take it from you.

At nationals, the scope and complexity increase dramatically compared to regionals. The networks are larger, the services more diverse, the red team more aggressive, and the business requirements more demanding. Success requires the entire team to operate at a high level simultaneously.

My responsibilities include managing cross-platform environments spanning Windows Server, Linux distributions, and network appliances. I build and execute incident response playbooks under live attack conditions, adapting them in real time as the situation evolves. I also mentor newer team members on defensive techniques and competition strategy.

The skills developed through CCDC competition translate directly to professional security operations. Incident response, system hardening, threat detection, and team coordination under pressure are the same skills required in a SOC, an IR team, or any security engineering role.
