## Sandia Labs Hackathon

Competed in cybersecurity hackathons organized by Sandia National Laboratories, tackling offensive and defensive security challenges designed by national laboratory researchers.

### What It Is

Sandia Labs hackathons are intensive cybersecurity events that blend capture-the-flag challenges with real-world problem solving. Challenges span vulnerability research, exploit development, reverse engineering, cryptography, and system security, all designed to test depth of understanding rather than surface-level knowledge.

### Achievements

Won top placements through systematic enumeration, creative exploitation techniques, and deep technical analysis of challenge environments.

### What It Demonstrates

National lab hackathons operate at a level above typical university CTFs. The challenges are designed by researchers who work on some of the most complex security problems in the country. Success requires not just technical skill but the ability to think creatively about attack surfaces, reason about system behavior, and persist through problems that do not have obvious solutions.

### Key Skills Applied

- Vulnerability enumeration and exploitation
- Binary analysis and reverse engineering
- Web application security testing
- Cryptographic analysis and attack implementation
- Network reconnaissance and pivoting
- Creative problem solving under time constraints
- Systematic methodology for unknown challenge types

### Why It Matters

These hackathons represent some of the most challenging competitive cybersecurity events available to students. Performing well against problems designed by national laboratory researchers validates both technical depth and the ability to tackle problems at the edge of current knowledge.
