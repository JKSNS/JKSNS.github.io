## RMCCDC Infrastructure Defense

RMCCDC (Rocky Mountain Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition) is a regional cybersecurity competition where student teams defend live enterprise infrastructure against professional red team attackers in real time.

### What It Is

RMCCDC tasks teams with inheriting a poorly secured corporate network and hardening it while maintaining business services. The competition simulates a realistic scenario: you are the new IT/security team for a company, the network is in bad shape, and attackers are already probing.

Teams must:
- Secure Windows and Linux servers, Active Directory, DNS, web applications, and databases
- Maintain service uptime and respond to business tasks (injects) from simulated management
- Detect, contain, and recover from active intrusions by a professional red team
- Document incidents and communicate findings under time pressure

### Achievements

Multiple first-place finishes at the Rocky Mountain regional level. Advanced to National CCDC to compete against the best collegiate teams in the country.

### What It Demonstrates

This competition tests the full spectrum of defensive security skills under extreme pressure. There is no script, no playbook that survives first contact, and no second chances. Success requires strong fundamentals, rapid adaptation, clear communication, and the ability to prioritize ruthlessly when everything is on fire.

### Key Skills Applied

- Active Directory hardening and monitoring
- Linux server security and service configuration
- Firewall rule management and network segmentation
- Incident response and threat containment
- Log analysis and forensic investigation
- Patch management under adversarial conditions
- Team coordination and communication under pressure

### Why It Matters

Competitions strip away the theoretical and expose what you actually know. RMCCDC forced me to build security instincts that classroom exercises cannot replicate. Every technique I use in real-world security work traces back to lessons learned defending networks under live fire.
